\itm Show that if $S$ is a square in the plane with sides parallel to the
axes and $A = \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & \lambda \\ 0 & 1 \end{array}
\right)$, then $AS$ has the same area as $S$.

\sol{If $S$ has opposite corners at $(x,y)$ and $(x+h,y+h)$, then $A$ maps
  $S$ to the parallelogram with opposite corners at $(x+\lambda y, y)$ and
  $(x+h+\lambda(y+h),y+h)$. This parallelogram has a base of $(x+h+\lambda y
  - (x + \lambda y) = h$ and a height of $y+h - y = h$, so its area is
  $h^2$. Since the area of $S$ is also $h^2$, we conclude that $AS$ and $S$
  have the same area. Note: $A$ is an example of a \textit{shear}
  transformation.}{}

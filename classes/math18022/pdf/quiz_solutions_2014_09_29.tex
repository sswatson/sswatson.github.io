\itm Find the limit as $(x,y) \to (0,0)$ of $\cos\left(\frac{x^3+y^3}{x^2+y^2}\right)$. 

\sol{Switching to polar coordinates, we find that the limit of the expression in parentheses is the same as the limit of $r^3(\cos^3\theta+\sin^3\theta)/r^2$ as $r\to 0$. Since the trigonometric expression is bounded and $r^3/r^2=r$ goes to 0 as $r$ goes to 0, we obtain a limit of 0 for the expression in parentheses. Since cosine is continuous, the limit of the given function is $\cos 0 = \boxed{1}$.}{\vspace{4cm}}
